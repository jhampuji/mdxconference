Overview

This symposium offers a vibrant platform for dialogue, reflection, and knowledge exchange among scholars, students, civil society organisers, and policy actors on the thematics of human rights, advocacy work, well-being initiatives and harm reduction strategies for LGBTQIA+ persons in Mauritius and the wider South-East African region.

At its heart, it seeks to advance knowledge, foster inclusive practices in research and project implementations, and promote wellbeing by weaving together academic expertise, community insights, legal and policy perspectives. Together, we aim to co-create actionable strategies that challenge systemic barriers and inspire collective action toward a more just and equitable society where every person is treated with dignity and respect.

Themes

Theme 1

Human Rights, Advocacy and Social Justice

  • National and international laws protecting LGBTQIA+ rights.
  • Lessons from LGBTQIA+ advocacy movements and initiatives.
  • Collective action, marriage equality and legal recognition for trans persons.
Theme 2

Public Attitudes on Sexual Orientation, Gender Identity and Expression

  • Societal perceptions of sexual orientation and gender identity.
  • Workplace policies, inclusion practices, risk management and safety of LGBTQIA+ persons.
  • Translation of legal reforms to LGBTQIA+ experiences: Benefits and Challenges.

Theme 3

LGBTQIA+ Identity and Intersectionality

  • Intersectional experiences of gender, sexuality, race, class, and disability.
  • Concealable vs Non-Concealable identities: lived experiences and resilience.
  • Opportunities and challenges within diverse regional communities.
Theme 4

Health and Wellbeing of LGBTQIA+ Persons

  • Mental health challenges and healthcare access for LGBTQIA+ communities.
  • Reality of gender-affirming care in a South-East African Context.
  • Strengths and gaps in regional health services and strategies for improvement.
Theme 5

Decolonizing Queerness

  • Indigenous, precolonial, and postcolonial queer perspectives.
  • Reclaiming regional queer histories and narratives.
  • Inclusive practices rooted in local and regional contexts.
