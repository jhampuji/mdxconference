LGBTQIA+ Perspectives: Symposium on Equality, Dignity and Wellbeing

The Symposium on Equality, Dignity and Wellbeing: LGBTQIA+ Perspectives, jointly organised by Young Queer Alliance and Middlesex University, and funded by The Other Foundation, will take place at Middlesex University Mauritius on 5 December 2025.

The symposium will feature panel discussions, presentations of the latest LGBTQIA+ research and community initiatives across the region. Local and regional scholars from South-East African countries, along with community leaders, are invited to submit abstracts or reports on community-driven work.

Themes

Human Rights, Advocacy and Social Justice
  • National and international laws protecting LGBTQIA+ rights
  • Lessons from advocacy movements and initiatives
  • Marriage equality and legal recognition for trans persons
Public Attitudes on Sexual Orientation, Gender Identity and Expression
  • Societal perceptions of sexual orientation and gender identity
  • Workplace inclusion practices, risk management and safety
  • Legal reforms: translating policy into lived experiences
LGBTQIA+ Identity and Intersectionality
  • Intersectional experiences of gender, sexuality, race, class, and disability
  • Concealable vs non-concealable identities and resilience
  • Diversity within regional communities
Health and Wellbeing of LGBTQIA+ Persons
  • Mental health and healthcare access
  • Gender-affirming care in a South-East African context
  • Regional health service gaps and strategies for improvement
Decolonizing Queerness
  • Indigenous, precolonial, and postcolonial queer perspectives
  • Reclaiming regional queer histories and narratives
  • Inclusive practices rooted in local contexts

Guidelines for Submissions

Abstracts
  • The abstract should include the following elements:
    • Introduction and background
    • Research question/objective/aim/purpose
    • Methodology
    • Findings
    • Contribution
    • The abstract should not exceed 500 words.
  • Maximum length: 500 words
Project Report Presentation (Community-driven Initiatives)
  • Can be a work in progress (max 500 words), including
    • Topic
    • Purpose for the project/initiative
    • Methodology/Execution Process
    • (Preliminary) Findings
    • Recommendations

All accepted abstracts will be published in the Book of Abstracts available on the symposium website and shared with participants after the symposium.
